The Accretion Rings

The rule that makes the ACI standard safe to build on: it grows only by accretion — you add a new member, you never revise a sealed one. Prior art can never break under you, because the past is never rewritten. Watch the family grow outward like tree-rings. Try to revise the core, and the standard refuses — it forks instead, and every old seal still holds.

The family, ring by ring · add, or try to revise

Two layers, honestly

Real
The data model is genuinely append-only. Each ring is sealed with a deterministic hash of its content at birth. “Add” pushes a new outer ring and touches nothing inner — press “verify” after any growth and every prior seal still recomputes green. “Revise” is structurally refused: it cannot mutate a sealed ring, so it forks a new one (the old ring stays, sealed, intact). Growth never invalidates the past — that is the property, and it is enforced, not promised.
